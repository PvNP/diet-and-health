Does National Diet Predict Disease Mortality Rates?

by Hansy Piou and Ben Zheng

Overview

Introduction and Motivation

We've all heard of the saying "You are what you eat" but how well does this adage hold up against cold hard facts (and data)? Though widely discussed in an informal context, there are few long-term studies showing how diet in uences well-being. Our project brought a data science approach to this important issue, focused on using countries' historical food consumption data to predict diabetes and cardiovascular disease mortality rate for that country. Using a wide range of countries, food groups and years of data collection to build our models, we hoped to find results supporting the notion that past diet a ects present health on a national level.
